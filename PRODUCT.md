# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary: someone mid-crisis. Divorce papers just arrived, custody dispute escalating, a parent just died and the estate is contested. They are stressed, possibly sleep-deprived, and Googling from their phone at 11pm. They need to feel that calling this firm won't make things harder. Secondary register exists: a future client portal or booking tool (product surface), but the dominant conversion surface is the marketing site.

## Product Purpose

Hagmans Familjerättsbyrå is a Stockholm-area family law firm. The site's job is to turn a scared person into a booked consultation. Success looks like: visitor lands, exhales, dials or clicks "Boka samtal". Secondary success: establishes credibility so a referred lead confirms they are calling the right people.

## Brand Personality

Honest, fearless, clear. The firm says hard things plainly. No hedging, no legal mystification, no false warmth. They are on the client's side — not in a cheerleader way, but in a "we will fight for you and tell you the truth" way.

## Anti-references

Big Swedish law firms: marble-texture headers, gold-seal logos, three-letter firm names, hero images of suited partners shaking hands. That world signals expense, inaccessibility, and corporate coldness. Also avoid legal-tech startup aesthetics: pastel SaaS gradients, floating avatars, "disrupting the legal industry" copy.

## Design Principles

1. **Relief over authority.** Every design decision asks: does this reduce the visitor's stress or increase it? Hierarchy and confidence matter, but they serve calm, not prestige.
2. **Radical plainness.** Plain language, plain layout, plain prices. Complexity is what clients are fleeing. The site mirrors the service: no hidden layers.
3. **The human name.** Real people, real names, real quotes. The founders' faces and words should outweigh any brand abstraction.
4. **Earned boldness.** The brutalist visual language earns attention by being different from every law firm, not by shouting. Scale and contrast carry weight; decoration does not.
5. **Speed to action.** The path from landing to booking should require zero friction. Every page is ultimately a ramp to the consultation CTA.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG level mandated. Apply WCAG 2.1 AA as a working baseline. Reduced-motion support already implemented in globals.css. Visitors may be emotionally distressed — avoid anything that feels aggressive, fast-flashing, or cognitively demanding.
